The green bars mark the Taobao firms with credit approval, and the (upper) red bars represent those without credit approval. The gray-shaded region marks the sampling interval for the FRDD with the discontinuity at the credit score of 480. Panel B is the discontinuity plot for the probability of credit approval against credit scores. The vertical axis is the probability of credit access. The horizontal axis is the credit score in the local range of [470, 490]. Each dot on the figure represents the average probability that a credit line is granted to a firm located in the credit score range with a bandwidth of two. The probability is estimated by dividing the total number of firms with credit access by the total number of eligible firms in the same bin. A linear line is fit to the scattered dots on each side of the cutoff score (i.e., 480), surrounded by a 95% confidence interval in light gray lines. Abstract  Based on automated credit lines to vendors trading on Alibaba's online retail platform and a discontinuity in the credit decision algorithm, we document that a vendor's access to FinTech credit boosts its sales growth, transaction growth, and the level of customer satisfaction gauged by product, service, and consignment ratings. These effects are more pronounced for vendors characterized by greater information asymmetry about their credit risk and less collateral, which reveals the information advantage of FinTech credit over traditional credit technology.
